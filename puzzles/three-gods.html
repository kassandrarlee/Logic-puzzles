The Three Gods Puzzle

A puzzle about removing layers of uncertainty in the right order.

Scenario

Three gods—True, False, and Random—stand before you.

  • True always answers truthfully.
  • False always answers falsely.
  • Random answers randomly, determined by a hidden coin flip before each question.

The gods understand English, but they answer every yes-or-no question using the words "ja" and "da." You do not know which word means yes and which means no.

You may ask exactly three yes-or-no questions, each directed to a single god of your choosing.

Can you determine the identity of all three gods?
Hint 1 — The Language
What is the first obstacle you need to overcome before you can gather any reliable information?
Reveal

The first obstacle is the language itself. Since you do not know whether "ja" means yes or no, you cannot immediately interpret any answer. Your first goal is therefore to find a way to ask questions whose meaning does not depend on knowing the language.

Hint 2 — Uncertainty
Once that obstacle has been addressed, what remaining uncertainty limits how much information you can trust?
Reveal

One of the gods does not answer consistently. As long as that source of uncertainty remains, every answer could be misleading. Identifying a consistently reliable source of information greatly simplifies the rest of the puzzle.

Hint 3 — The Remaining Information
Once those two obstacles have been removed, what information is still needed to completely solve the puzzle?
Reveal

With the language ambiguity addressed and the unreliable source isolated, the remaining questions can distinguish the two consistently answering gods and resolve the final uncertainty.

Complete Explanation

The Three Gods Puzzle is unusual because it is not built around a single insight. Instead, it requires solving several smaller problems, each of which removes one layer of uncertainty.

The first challenge is the unknown language. Before any answer can be interpreted, the questions themselves must be designed so that their meaning does not depend on knowing whether "ja" means yes or no. This is accomplished using carefully constructed self-referential questions.

Once the language ambiguity has been neutralized, the next priority is isolating the god whose answers cannot be trusted. Eliminating this uncertainty allows the remaining questions to be directed toward consistently answering gods, making each response much more informative.

With those obstacles removed, the remaining questions distinguish the truthful and deceptive gods while resolving the final uncertainty.

There is no single required sequence of questions. Several different three-question strategies have been developed, all based on the same underlying principles.

Rather than memorizing one particular solution, the important insight is recognizing the order in which the obstacles are removed:

  1. Neutralize the language ambiguity.
  2. Isolate the unreliable source of information.
  3. Use the remaining reliable answers to identify each god.

The elegance of the puzzle lies not in a single clever question, but in the way each question removes one obstacle and makes the next question more informative.
