Interactive puzzle collection

Logic & Reasoning

A curated collection of classic puzzles in logic, probability, and game theory.

These puzzles are presented in a guided format designed to encourage exploration rather than simply revealing solutions.

Each begins with the full scenario, then unfolds through a sequence of steps before revealing the complete explanation and underlying principle.

Explore as you'd like by working through the guides or jumping directly to the explanations!

Logic

Puzzles about knowledge, certainty, truth, and the structure of deduction.

Blue-Eyed Islanders What everyone knows, and what everyone knows that everyone knows. → Counterfeit Coin How much information can one weighing reveal? → Three Gods Truth, falsehood, randomness, and carefully chosen questions. →

Probability

Puzzles where intuition and probability diverge.

Monty Hall Problem How information changes probability. → Secretary Problem How to make the best choice when the future is unavailable. → Guess 2/3 of the Average Reasoning through the reasoning of others. → Poisoned Wine Encoding many possibilities into a small number of tests. →

Strategy & Coordination

Puzzles and games where success depends on planning, coordination, or anticipating others.

100 Prisoners and the Lightbulb How a group can coordinate with almost no communication. → 100 Prisoners and 50 Boxes A strategy hidden inside a permutation. → Ultimatum Game Where logic and human behavior part ways. →

About This Collection

The puzzles presented here are classic problems from mathematics, logic, probability, and game theory that have been explored and discussed for many years.

This collection, including its organization, explanations, guided questions, and presentation, was curated and written by Kassandra Lee, PhD.
